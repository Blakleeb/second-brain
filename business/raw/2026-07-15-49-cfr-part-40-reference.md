Want 49 CFR Part 40 in the vault — it's the federal rule that governs all DOT drug and alcohol testing procedures. This is the cornerstone compliance reference for BBB.

Key thing I learned checking for updates (July 2026): DOT published a new final rule May 11, 2026, effective June 10, 2026. It lets us keep using directly observed URINE collections in situations that would otherwise call for an oral fluid test — because oral fluid testing still isn't actually usable (no HHS-certified oral fluid labs online yet). So urine remains the method.

Also: DOT swapped "gender" for "sex" in parts of the rule, fixed an observer-vs-collector wording error in 40.67(g), and there's a PROPOSED (not final) rule to add fentanyl + norfentanyl to the testing panel — worth watching.
