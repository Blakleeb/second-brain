# Post-Accident Testing Rules

Post-accident is one of the [[DOT Test Reasons — The 6 Types]] and one of the trickiest for customers to get right — the time windows are tight and the "which accidents qualify" rules are specific. FMCSA post-accident rules live in **§ 382.303**.

## Time windows (memorize these)

- **Alcohol test:** administer within **2 hours** if at all possible; **hard stop at 8 hours**. If not done within 8 hours, stop trying and document why.
- **Drug (urine) test:** administer within **32 hours**. If not done within 32 hours, stop trying and document why.

The employer must document any reason a required test wasn't completed in time.

## Which accidents require a test

A DOT post-accident test is required when one of these is true:

1. **Fatality** — a human fatality resulted from the accident. **Always test.** (No citation needed.)
2. **Injury + citation** — the driver got a citation for a moving violation, AND someone involved received **immediate** medical treatment away from the scene.
3. **Disabling damage + citation** — the driver got a citation for a moving violation, AND a vehicle was **towed** from the scene.

## Key clarification — "immediate"

The rule hinges on *immediate* medical attention away from the scene. If a person only *later* drives themselves to the ER after the fact, post-accident testing is **not** triggered by that.

## Why this matters to BBB

- Post-accident calls are time-sensitive — BBB's mobile model ("we roll to you") is a real advantage when the 2-hour alcohol clock is ticking.
- BBB has **missed a few post-accident calls** early on (see [[BBB Business Facts]]) — fast response here is a growth and reputation lever.
- Knowing the qualification rules lets BBB help a panicked employer figure out fast whether a test is even required.
