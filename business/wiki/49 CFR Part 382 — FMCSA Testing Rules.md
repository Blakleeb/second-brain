# 49 CFR Part 382 — FMCSA Testing Rules

**49 CFR Part 382** is the FMCSA regulation that governs controlled-substance and alcohol testing for **CDL drivers** operating commercial motor vehicles. Where [[49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures]] defines *how* a test is conducted, Part 382 defines *who* must be tested, *when*, and *how often*. This is the rule most of BBB's trucking and motor-carrier customers live under.

## Who it covers

Every CDL holder operating a commercial motor vehicle (CMV) that requires a commercial driver's license, and the employers (motor carriers) who employ them. Drivers already covered by FTA testing under Part 655 are excepted (§ 382.103(d)(1)).

## The six testing situations

Part 382, Subpart C requires testing in six situations — see [[DOT Test Reasons — The 6 Types]]:
pre-employment, random, post-accident, reasonable suspicion, return-to-duty, and follow-up.

## Random testing rates (current)

- **Drugs: 50% minimum** annual rate of the average number of driver positions.
- **Alcohol: 10% minimum** annual rate.
- FMCSA can lower the drug rate to **25%** only after the industry positive rate stays below 1.0% for two consecutive years; it returns to 50% if the rate climbs back above that threshold.
- A new minimum rate takes effect January 1 of the year following its Federal Register publication.

## What employers must do

- Test in all six situations above.
- Run [[FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse]] queries (pre-employment full query + annual limited query).
- Keep drivers with a positive/refusal out of safety-sensitive duty until the return-to-duty process is complete.
