# 49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures

**49 CFR Part 40** is the federal regulation that governs *how* drug and alcohol testing must be conducted for the entire transportation industry — every DOT mode (FMCSA, FAA, FRA, FTA, PHMSA, and the Coast Guard) points back to it. For BBB Mobile DOT Drug Testing this is the cornerstone compliance reference: it defines collector duties, specimen handling, chain of custody, the role of the MRO, and the return-to-duty process.

Issued and maintained by the DOT Office of Drug and Alcohol Policy and Compliance (ODAPC).

## Current testing methods (as of July 2026)

- **Urine** is the working method — 5-panel urine collection is standard.
- **Oral fluid** testing was *added* to Part 40 on May 2, 2023, but **still cannot be used**. There are no HHS-certified oral fluid laboratories online yet, so employers can't run oral fluid tests under Part 40.

## What changed — 2026 final rule

DOT published a final rule on **May 11, 2026**, effective **June 10, 2026**:

- **Directly observed urine stays allowed** in situations that would otherwise require an oral fluid test, *as long as oral fluid testing isn't available.* This is an interim measure to keep testing running until oral fluid labs exist.
- **"Available" is now defined:** oral fluid testing only counts as available when all three exist — at least two HHS-certified oral fluid labs, a qualified oral fluid collector, and a conforming collection device at the site.
- **Terminology:** "gender" replaced with "sex" in certain provisions (to conform with E.O. 14168).
- **Drafting fix:** corrected an "observer" vs "collector" error in § 40.67(g).

**Bottom line for BBB:** urine-based collections (including directly observed where required) are current and compliant.

## On the horizon — not final yet

A **proposed** rule would add **fentanyl and norfentanyl** to the DOT drug testing panel, harmonizing with HHS guidelines for urine and oral fluid. Still in the proposal stage — watch the Federal Register page for movement.
