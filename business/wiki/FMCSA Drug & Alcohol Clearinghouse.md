# FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse

The **FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse** is the federal online database of CDL driver drug-and-alcohol program violations. Employers of CDL drivers are required to use it, and it sits alongside [[49 CFR Part 382 — FMCSA Testing Rules]] in every motor carrier's compliance stack.

## What employers must do

- **Pre-employment full query** — run a *full* query (requires the driver's electronic consent via the Clearinghouse) before a new driver performs any safety-sensitive function.
- **Annual limited query** — run at least one *limited* query per driver every 12 months. The deadline is **January 5** each year for the prior calendar year.
- **Report violations within 24 hours** — employers report positive results, refusals, and SAP completions within 24 hours. Labs and MROs submit results promptly.
- **Registration** — carriers with a USDOT number manage Clearinghouse Administrator/Assistant roles through the FMCSA Portal.

## Key changes (Clearinghouse II)

- If a full query returns **no violations**, employers are **no longer required** to separately request testing history from a driver's previous employers.
- State DMVs now query the Clearinghouse before issuing or renewing a CDL.
- Drivers with a **"prohibited"** status have their CDL **downgraded** to a standard license; states must complete the downgrade within 60 days of notification.

## Penalties

Allowing a driver with prohibited status to operate a CMV can bring penalties up to **$10,000 per violation, per day**.

## Why this matters to BBB

Customers frequently conflate "testing" with "Clearinghouse compliance." Knowing the query rules lets BBB speak to a carrier's full obligation, not just the collection. (BBB performs collections; Clearinghouse registration/queries are the employer's responsibility — a natural talking point.)
