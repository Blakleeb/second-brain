# SAP & Return-to-Duty Process

When a driver fails or refuses a DOT test, they're pulled from safety-sensitive duty until they complete the **return-to-duty (RTD)** process run by a **Substance Abuse Professional (SAP)**. This is governed by [[49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures]], **Subpart O**. Two of the [[DOT Test Reasons — The 6 Types]] — return-to-duty and follow-up — come out of this process.

## Who the SAP is

A SAP is a qualified professional who evaluates the employee and prescribes education/treatment. Importantly, the SAP is **not** an advocate for the employer or the employee — their job is to protect public safety. (BBB is a collector, not a SAP; this is reference for advising customers.)

## The process, in order

1. **Violation** — positive test or refusal → driver removed from safety-sensitive duty.
2. **SAP evaluation** — comprehensive assessment and clinical evaluation.
3. **Education / treatment** — SAP prescribes it; employee must successfully comply.
4. **Follow-up (re-)evaluation** — SAP confirms compliance.
5. **Return-to-duty test** — **directly observed**; must be negative before the driver returns.
6. **Follow-up testing plan** — the SAP sets an unannounced schedule.

## Follow-up testing plan

- **At least 6 follow-up tests in the first 12 months** after returning.
- Can extend up to **5 years** total, at the SAP's discretion.
- The **SAP alone** decides the number, frequency, and whether tests are for drugs, alcohol, or both.
- The plan **follows the employee** to new employers and through breaks in service.
- Return-to-duty and follow-up tests are **directly observed** collections — see the directly-observed provisions in [[49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures]].

## Why this matters to BBB

- RTD and follow-up tests are recurring, scheduled, directly observed collections — a **steady, contract-friendly** revenue type that fits BBB's goal of moving from one-off wins to recurring contracts (see [[BBB Business Facts]]).
- Employers often don't understand that follow-up obligations "follow the driver" — a useful thing BBB can explain.
