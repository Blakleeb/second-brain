# Industry Associations & Collector Standards

The testing industry's standards bodies and the collector-qualification rules that back BBB's professionalism. BBB is a **member of NDASA**.

## NDASA — National Drug & Alcohol Screening Association

The primary trade association for the drug- and alcohol-screening industry. **DATIA (Drug & Alcohol Testing Industry Association) merged into NDASA in June 2023**, so NDASA now administers the certification programs DATIA previously offered. If older material references "DATIA CPC," that program is now run by NDASA.

> Note: Blaklee referred to this as "NADSA / NDA (National Drug Association)" — these all point to **NDASA**. Keep the spelling **NDASA** on outward-facing material.

## Collector certifications (CPC / CPCT)

- **CPC — Certified Professional Collector.** The collector signs an agreement to adhere to the training standards; the administrator issues a form documenting the collector as **USDOT Qualified and a CPC**. Valid for **five years** (matching DOT's requirement), with an annual CPC renewal exam to keep the NDASA designation current.
- **CPCT — Certified Professional Collector Trainer.** Must maintain current collector certification and pass an annual CPCT exam.

## The critical language distinction — "Qualified," not "Certified"

**The DOT does not "certify" collectors.** Under [[49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures]], a collector becomes **DOT Qualified** by completing the required qualification training and error-correction/proficiency demonstration. CPC/CPCT are *industry* certifications from NDASA — separate from DOT qualification.

➡️ **Always say "DOT Qualified," never "DOT Certified."** This is both technically correct and a BBB house rule. See [[BBB Business Facts]].

## Why this matters to BBB

- BBB's credibility rests on being **DOT Qualified** (collector + BAT) plus NDASA membership — real, verifiable standards, not marketing fluff.
- Correct terminology signals expertise to informed buyers (TPAs, safety managers) and avoids the compliance red flag of claiming "certification" DOT doesn't grant.
