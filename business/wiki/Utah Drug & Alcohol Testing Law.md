# Utah Drug & Alcohol Testing Law

Utah's state framework for workplace testing is the **Utah Drug and Alcohol Testing Act — Utah Code Title 34, Chapter 38**. It governs *non-DOT* employer testing in Utah. (DOT-regulated testing still runs under the federal rules — see [[49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures]] and [[49 CFR Part 382 — FMCSA Testing Rules]].) BBB serves Summit, Wasatch, and Salt Lake counties — this is the state law behind BBB's non-DOT 5-panel work.

## How the Utah law works (it's a "safe harbor")

Utah's law is largely **voluntary and protective**: an employer that tests *in accordance with Chapter 38* gets liability protection. A cause of action generally can't arise against an employer who runs a compliant testing program and acts on the results — **unless** the action was based on an inaccurate test result.

## Core requirements for employers

- **Written policy required.** Testing must be done under a written policy distributed to employees and available to prospective employees.
- **Permitted purposes.** Investigating individual impairment; investigating workplace accidents or theft; maintaining safety of employees/public; maintaining productivity, product/service quality, or security of property/information.
- **Timing & pay.** Testing of current employees happens during or right after the regular work period and counts as work time for pay/benefits.
- **Employer pays.** The employer covers all testing costs it requires, including transportation if a current employee is tested off-site — relevant to BBB's mobile "we roll to you" model.
- **Sanitary, controlled collection.** Samples collected under reasonable, sanitary conditions designed to prevent substitution or tampering.

## Special case — high-level nuclear waste

Organizations storing, transferring, or transporting high-level or greater-than-class-C radioactive waste in Utah must run a **mandatory** drug/alcohol testing program for prospective and existing employees, and management must periodically test themselves.

## Why this matters to BBB

- BBB's **non-DOT 5-panel** testing operates under this state framework; DOT tests do not.
- The "employer must have a written policy" requirement is a natural value-add BBB can point customers toward.
- The mobile/off-site cost-coverage rule aligns with how BBB already operates.
