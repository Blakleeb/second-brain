# DOT Test Reasons — The 6 Types

Under [[49 CFR Part 382 — FMCSA Testing Rules]] (and mirrored across the other DOT modes), there are exactly **six reasons** a DOT drug or alcohol test is conducted. Every test BBB performs falls into one of these categories.

1. **Pre-employment** — before a driver first performs a safety-sensitive function for an employer. Drug test required; a negative result must be received before the driver can perform safety-sensitive duties. A pre-employment [[FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse]] full query is also required.

2. **Random** — unannounced, spread throughout the year, drawn from the employer's testing pool. Minimum annual rates: **50% drugs, 10% alcohol** (FMCSA). Selection must be truly random and each driver has an equal chance each selection period.

3. **Post-accident** — after qualifying crashes (e.g., a fatality, or a citation plus injury/tow-away, depending on the mode's thresholds). Alcohol testing has tight time windows (generally within 8 hours); drug testing within 32 hours.

4. **Reasonable suspicion** — when a trained supervisor observes specific, articulable signs of drug or alcohol use (appearance, behavior, speech, odor). Must be documented.

5. **Return-to-duty (RTD)** — required before a driver who violated the rules can return to safety-sensitive work, after completing the Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) process. RTD tests are **directly observed**.

6. **Follow-up** — an unannounced testing schedule set by the SAP after return-to-duty (at least 6 tests in the first 12 months, can extend up to 5 years). Also **directly observed**.

## Notes for BBB operations

- Pre-employment, random, and post-accident are the bread-and-butter for most customers.
- RTD and follow-up tests are directly observed collections — see the directly-observed provisions in [[49 CFR Part 40 — DOT Testing Procedures]].
- BBB offers DOT urine (5-panel) and DOT breath alcohol (BAT); see [[BBB Business Facts]].
