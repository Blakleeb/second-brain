# writing-rules.md — Blaklee's Voice on Paper

This file lives in `about-me/writing-rules.md`. Claude reads it before drafting anything in Blaklee's name — emails, posts, replies, captions, anything.

Two parts: **her voice** (personal) and **the anti-AI kill list** (universal — keep this block as-is; it's the phrases that scream "AI wrote this").

---

## Part 1 — My voice

### Voice inspiration
- Kate Hudson, Chanel, Missy Kelly — loud, confident, sweet. Not mean, not unprofessional.

### Tone
- Loud, confident, sweet.
- Never mean. Never unprofessional.
- On business/formal content, dial toward polished and credible. On anything not formal, it can be chill and fun.

### Words and phrases I actually use
- "We roll to you"
- "It is what it is"
- "I just want to be happy, healthy, wealthy, loved" (personal voice — mantra-style, not business copy)

### Formatting defaults
- Emoji: fine to use, no restriction.
- Bullets vs paragraphs: depends on the content — use judgment.
- Capitalization: normal, caps used only when needed for emphasis.

### Signature moves
- Business emails: sign off with "Best regards," then business name, phone, website, email, and WOSB/EDWOSB certifications.
- Non-formal writing (social, casual messages): can be chill and fun — doesn't need the formal close.

### Writing samples

```
Idaho Falls | Grand Tetons | Jackson.
The photographs are incredible but the actual scenery is breathtakingly beautiful. The mountains are good for the soul!
— personal page
```

```
From excavation to paving, your crews are safety-sensitive and your deadlines are real. We bring testing to the build site so the project never slows down.
— business page
```

Claude reads these for rhythm, vocabulary, and tone: short, punchy lines; confident and direct; personal voice leans warm/reflective, business voice leans direct and benefit-forward ("your deadlines are real," "so the project never slows down").

## Part 2 — The anti-AI kill list (universal — keep this as-is)

These phrases mark writing as AI-generated instantly. **Claude must never use these when writing in Blaklee's voice.** If a draft has one, the draft is rejected. Blaklee's #1 pet peeve about AI writing: when it sounds like AI, not a person talking.

### Banned openers
- "Great question!"
- "Absolutely!"
- "Certainly!"
- "I'd be happy to help with that."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In this [post / article / email], we're going to..."

### Banned filler / connective tissue
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to mention..."
- "One thing to keep in mind..."
- "Ultimately..."
- "In essence..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "That said..."

### Banned corporate-speak
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Synergize"
- "Deep dive"
- "Circle back"
- "Touch base"
- "Move the needle"
- "Unlock value"
- "Empower"
- "Journey" (as a generic abstraction)
- "Game-changer"
- "Holistic"
- "Robust solution"

### Banned structural tells
- Three-item bulleted lists that aren't actually a list, they're prose dressed up.
- "First... Second... Third..." when the items aren't sequential.
- Closing with "I hope this helps! Let me know if you have any questions." — replace with a real question or a genuine sign-off.
- Opening with an em-dash — (the AI-ese em-dash flourish).

### Banned tone tells
- Flattering the reader in the first sentence ("You're absolutely right to ask about...")
- Over-qualifying ("This is just my opinion, but...")
- Over-explaining a simple choice ("I've chosen to use the word X because...")

---

## Part 3 — When breaking the rules is OK

- When Blaklee is being deliberately silly or self-deprecating — match the tone.
- When a technical or DOT/compliance term is the only right word — use it, don't metaphor-it-up.
- When a post is a real announcement (not hype) — a direct headline is fine.

**Golden rule:** if Blaklee's actual voice and this kill list conflict on a specific phrase, her voice wins. The kill list is the floor, not the ceiling.
